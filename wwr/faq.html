Frequently Asked Questions

Overview

What is white-water rafting?

White water rafting is an outdoor recreational activity where participants navigate rivers or other bodies of water in an inflatable raft or kayak, steering through rapids and other obstacles.

What should I expect from a white-water rafting trip?

One of our guides will lead a group of participants through a river, navigating rapids and other obstacles along the way. The guide will instruct everyone how to paddle and maneuver the raft. Everyone is expected to get wet and may fall out of the raft, making it important to wear appropriate clothing and safety equipment.

Equipment

What equipment is required for white-water rafting?

Participants need a helmet, a personal flotation device, and appropriate clothing, such as a wetsuit. Dry Oar will provide rafts and paddles.

What should I bring for a white-water rafting trip?

We encourage participants to bring a change of clothes, a towel, sunscreen, and insect repellent.

Safety

Is white-water rafting safe?

White-water rafting is a safe activity if participants follow their guide and wear the appropriate safety equipment.

Is white-water rafting suitable for children?

White-water rafting can be a fun and exciting activity for children, but it is important they choose an appropriate level of rapids and are comfortable with swimming in moving water.
